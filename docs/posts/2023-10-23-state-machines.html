Posted on October 23, 2023

Mealy machines are functions m:s×i→s×o or, equivalently, a pair of functions m₀:s×i→s, m₁:s×i→o. s is the state space, i is the input alphabet, and o is the output alphabet.

In some important applications s,i,o are finite, e.g., regexp lexers.

A Mealy machine gives rise to a graph π,m₀:s×i⇉s. In case s is finite this can be drawn:

In other applications, they are infinite, or at least too large to draw easily.
