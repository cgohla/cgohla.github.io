Highlight: The Categorical Origins of Lebesgue Integration

Posted on December 31, 2021

I want to discuss a recent pre-print by Tom Leinster, namely [[https://arxiv.org/abs/2011.00412][The categorical origins of Lebesgue integration]].

It is a very nice exposition of some very important gadgets in functional analysis, namely the vector spaces Lp([0,1]) and ℓp. The part that really caught interest is that the aforementioned spaces are initial F-algebras in normed vector spaces.

Let me explain why this is nice: (F)-algebras are a very basic concept from categorical algebra: if F is a functor F : C → C, then an F-algebra on an object X is a C-morphism ξ : FX → X. A morphism $f: X → Y $ is an F-algebra morphism if υ ∘ Ff = f ∘ ξ.

A simple example over sets would be the functor X ↦ 1 + X. In this case an algebra on a set X is a pair of functions 1 → X and X → X. It’s easy to see that the natural numbers N are such an algebra, where the first function picks the number 0, and the second is the successor function.

What makes the natural numbers special among all algebras of this kind is that for any other such algebra α : 1 + A → A there is a unique function f : N → A, which is an algebra homomorphism. This is essentially a restatement of the Peano axioms.

Categorically we say that 1 + N → N is initial in the category of (1+_)-algebras.

Now let us turn our attention to the category Ban of Banach spaces and contractive maps, i.e., linear maps of norm at most 1. Let’s say the ground field is ℝ

The field ℝ itself is of course a Banach space in a natural way, so it is an object in Ban. A morphism R → V to another Banach space corresponds to a choice of vector v in V of norm at most 1. In particular, such Banach spaces with a choice of vector of norm less than 1 form a category ℝ/Ban where the morphisms are linear contractions taking chosen vector to chosen vector. This cateogry has an initial object, namely ℝ with chosen element 1, meaning there is a unique linear contraction (ℝ,1) → (V,v) for every (V,v).

We are now going to define an endo-functor Fp on Ban and extend it to ℝ/Ban where p ≥ 1 is a real number: Fp takes the vector space V to the direct sum V ⊕ V with the norm given by the p-average $$|v+v'| = \left(\frac{1}{2}(|v|^p+|v'|^p)\right)^{\frac{1}{p}}$$.

To extend Fp to $\mathb{R}/\mathrm{Ban}$ we observe that if our chosen vector in V is v then in V ⊕ V we can choose v + v, and its norm will be exactly |v| with the norm taken in V. If the vector space in question was ℝ, then Fp of it is ℝ2 with the (appropiately scaled) p-norm. Because (ℝ,1) is initial in our category, The diagonal ℝ → ℝ2 is the uniquely determined contractive linear map.

If we keep iterating Fp we get Fn(ℝ,1) = (ℝ2n,e) where e is all 1s and the norm is the p-average over all the entries in a vector. For convenince let’s En = Fn(ℝ,1). We get norm preserving linear maps En → En + 1 that duplicate every entry in a row vector.
